## Brainstorm and Affinity Diagram

Going into the brainstorming process, we had a rather clear idea of the features and issues that we would need to address. Thus when we met over the course of several days we discussed our solutions to solving those issues as well as arriving at new solutions as a group by either rehashing existing ideas or generating something completely new. Also, despite being partily unintentional, meeting over several days allowed us to sleep on the ideas and features we wished to eventually implement in our application. 
 
The main purpose of our sketches during this process was to better explain and display our ideas to the group. Although those sketched were also used for ensuring consensus around an idea and to serve as an archive of what we had already thought of. Overall we tried to come up with at least two or three completely unique systems or layouts for each of the major components of our application. That way we were less likely to get stuck on a single set of ideas. Of course if we had more time we’d have attempted to come up with far more unique ideas than just two to three for each major component. 
 
Of course for sketching we needed paper and pencils but otherwise we basically didn’t use anything else. I suppose a whiteboard would have come in handy for quickly sketching things as a group but that would have been harder to archive for later parts of the assignment such as the video prototype or storyboard. As such we sketched our sketched on pieces of paper that we could easily reuse in the video prototype and storyboard.
 
For better or for worse throughout the processes we sometimes ended up mixing and matching elements from existing ideas when coming up with new ideas rather than coming up with completely new solutions. Although despite that we were still able to come up with completely novel ideas.
